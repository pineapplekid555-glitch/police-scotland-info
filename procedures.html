Police Scotland Procedures

Standard operating procedures and protocols used by Police Scotland

Community Policing Procedures

Objectives:

  • Build positive relationships with communities
  • Increase public confidence in policing
  • Address local concerns and issues
  • Provide support and assistance to vulnerable groups

Key Steps:

  1. Engage with community leaders and organizations
  2. Attend community events and meetings
  3. Respond to local concerns promptly
  4. Provide crime prevention advice
  5. Work with partners to address root causes of crime

Evidence Collection and Handling

Chain of Custody Requirements:

  • All evidence must be properly documented
  • Document the location where evidence was found
  • Record the officer who collected the evidence
  • Maintain a log of all persons who handle the evidence
  • Store evidence in secure, temperature-controlled facilities

Documentation:

  1. Photograph evidence in situ
  2. Complete incident report forms
  3. Label and seal all evidence containers
  4. Record details on evidence log sheets
  5. Maintain chain of custody documentation

Stop and Search Procedures

Legal Requirements:

  • Must have reasonable suspicion
  • Identify yourself as police officer
  • State the reason for the stop and search
  • Explain what you will be searching for
  • Inform of right to know your name and station

Procedure:

  1. Approach person respectfully
  2. Explain legal basis for search
  3. Conduct search in professional manner
  4. Respect dignity and privacy of individual
  5. Complete search record form
  6. Provide receipt to person if requested

Arrest and Custody Procedures

Arrest Process:

  • Identify yourself and state authority to arrest
  • State reason for arrest clearly
  • Use minimum force necessary
  • Inform of right to legal representation
  • Read rights and caution to suspect

Custody Rules:

  1. Record time of arrest in custody record
  2. Allow access to legal advisor
  3. Conduct risk assessment upon arrival
  4. Provide appropriate meals and refreshments
  5. Allow reasonable rest periods
  6. Conduct medical examination if necessary
  7. Record all interviews with solicitor present

Incident Report Writing

Report Components:

  • Date, time and location of incident
  • Details of persons involved
  • Factual account of what occurred
  • Actions taken by officers
  • Any charges or outcomes
  • Officer signature and badge number

Writing Standards:

  1. Write in clear, objective language
  2. Avoid opinions or speculation
  3. Document facts in chronological order
  4. Include all relevant witness information
  5. Proof-read for accuracy and spelling
  6. Submit within required timeframe

Mental Health Crisis Response

Approach:

  • Assess immediate danger to self or others
  • Call for mental health crisis team if needed
  • Communicate calmly and compassionately
  • Avoid sudden movements or loud noises
  • Maintain safe distance

Actions:

  1. Establish clear communication
  2. Listen without judgment
  3. Avoid confrontation
  4. Coordinate with health professionals
  5. Ensure appropriate care and support
  6. Document incident thoroughly
